## 1. Product Launch Formula (PLF) -- Complete Framework

### The Complete Launch Sequence (All Phases)

```
PRE-PRE-LAUNCH
    |
    v
PRE-LAUNCH (PLC 1 -> PLC 2 -> PLC 3)
    |
    v
LAUNCH (Open Cart -> Cart Period -> Close Cart)
    |
    v
POST-LAUNCH (Delivery + Debrief + Seed Next Launch)
```

### Phase 1: Pre-Pre-Launch

**Purpose**: Gauge interest, build initial buzz, gather intelligence
**Duration**: Days to weeks before prelaunch begins
**Key Concept**: "The Shot Across the Bow"

**Actions:**
- Send a teaser to your list that signals something is coming
- DO NOT appear to be selling anything directly
- Ask questions to engage dialogue ("What's your biggest struggle with X?")
- Use surveys to identify exact pain points
- Build anticipation without revealing the product
- Gather language your prospects use (for future copy)

**Mental Triggers Activated**: Anticipation, Community

[SOURCE: jeffwalker.com/product-launch-process/; "Launch" book; productlaunchstrategy.org]

---

### Phase 2: Pre-Launch (The Sideways Sales Letter)

**Purpose**: Deliver massive value, build trust, create desire
**Duration**: 7-10 days (3 pieces of content released sequentially)
**Key Concept**: "The Sideways Sales Letter" -- takes a traditional long-form sales letter and delivers it horizontally over time as high-value content

**Core Philosophy**: The job of prelaunch content is NOT to immediately get them to buy. It's to take your prospect on a journey revealing the opportunity, transformation, and experience -- content should be valuable whether or not they purchase.

**Path Structure**: Problem -> Solution -> Problem (PSP) -- "every solution holds the seeds to the next problem" (differs from traditional PAS -- Problem-Agitate-Solve)

#### PLC 1: "The Opportunity"

**Theme**: Show them what's possible
**Content Roadmap:**
- Show the opportunity and how their life is going to change
- Position yourself: show why they should listen to you (authority)
- Teach something valuable (reciprocity)
- Talk about passion, what people want most in life
- Build excitement about what's coming next

**Mental Triggers Activated**: Authority, Reciprocity, Anticipation

#### PLC 2: "The Transformation"

**Theme**: Show them the "what" -- how transformation happens
**Content Roadmap:**
- Thank people for comments and questions from PLC1
- Recap the opportunity from PLC1
- Remind them who you are and why they should listen
- Present a case study OR do real teaching (teach one cool thing they can use quickly)
- Address top 2-3 objections (objection crushing)
- Foreshadow PLC3, build anticipation for what will be taught next

**Mental Triggers Activated**: Social Proof, Trust, Anticipation

#### PLC 3: "The Ownership Experience"

**Theme**: What would it feel like to OWN the mastery
**Content Roadmap:**
- Explain the big view and how to make it happen
- Answer top questions and objections from PLC1 and PLC2
- Share the ownership experience -- what life looks like on the other side
- In the last ~25% of the content: pivot to your offer with a "soft landing"
- Prepare prospects for the upcoming offer (not hard sell, but clear transition)

**Mental Triggers Activated**: Anticipation, Community, Likability

[SOURCE: jeffwalker.com/product-launch-process/; productlaunchclass.com/sideways-sales-letter/; breakthroughmarketingsecrets.com; "Launch" book]

---

### Phase 3: Launch (Open Cart -> Close Cart)

**Purpose**: Convert interest into sales with urgency
**Duration**: 5-7 days typically

#### Open Cart

**Actions:**
- Send "The doors are open!" email
- Email your list EVERY DAY during Open Cart
- Remind them of the opportunity, transformation, and experience
- Use scarcity and urgency honestly:
  - "This offer is only open for 5 days"
  - "Next time it will be more expensive"
  - "This bonus vanishes tonight"
- Walker's position: "It's not sleazy or slimy" -- you're being honest about real deadlines

**Email Structure During Open Cart:**
- Day 1: "The doors are open!" (excitement, link to sales page)
- Day 2-4: Reminder emails + stories + testimonials + objection handling
- Day 5-7: Urgency escalation leading to Close Cart

#### Close Cart

**Duration**: Final day of the launch
**Emails (minimum 3 on final day):**

1. **Morning email**: Simple reminder that the deadline is tonight
2. **Midday email**: Longer, story-based email reminding why they want the offer and how they'll feel if they miss out
3. **Evening email**: Short and sweet -- "Hey! This closes in a few hours. It's now or never!"
4. **Optional final email**: "LAST CHANCE" email ~1 hour before deadline (only if maximizing)

**Mental Triggers Activated**: Scarcity, Urgency, Social Proof, Events/Rituals

[SOURCE: jeffwalker.com/product-launch-process/; systeme.io/blog/product-launch-summary; productlaunchclass.com]

---

### Phase 4: Post-Launch

**Purpose**: Fulfill, debrief, set up next launch
**Duration**: Ongoing after cart closes

**Actions:**
1. **Follow up with buyers**: Reinforce they made the right decision
2. **Help them consume the product**: Onboarding sequence to ensure success
3. **Follow up with non-buyers**: Re-engage without pressure
4. **Follow up with JV partners** (if applicable): Thank them, share results
5. **Encourage conversation**: "That conversation will give you the ideas for your next product"
6. **Debrief**: Walker has a fine-tuned debrief process analyzing what worked and what didn't
7. **Seed the next launch**: Begin the cycle again

**Key Principle**: "Give them what you promised, give them great customer support, and then give them an extra surprise or two along the way. Do those things and you will be paid back 100 times."

[SOURCE: jeffwalker.com/post-launch-routine-post/; jeffwalker.com/launch-debriefs-post/; "Launch" book]

## 2. Launch Types

### Seed Launch

**When to use**: No product, no list, no business, or testing a new idea
**How it works**:
- Offer live training to a small group
- Let buyers shape the content (co-creation)
- Record everything -> becomes the product
- Build your first list of prospects and buyers
- "You've been paid to create a product"

**Ideal for**: Beginners, new product ideas, pivots
[SOURCE: productlaunchstrategy.org/tag/seed-launch/; "Launch" book]

### Internal Launch

**When to use**: You have a list and a market-tested product
**How it works**:
- Full Sideways Sales Letter approach
- Launch to your own email list and following
- No JV partners needed
- Uses polished PLC sequence with early testimonials

**Ideal for**: Established businesses wanting bigger months
[SOURCE: productlaunchstrategy.org/tag/internal-launch/; jeffwalker.com/jv-launch-vs-internal-launch-post/]

### JV (Joint Venture) Launch

**When to use**: Proven offer, ready for exponential growth
**How it works**:
- Attract partners who serve the same audience with complementary products
- Partners promote your prelaunch content to their lists
- Requires trust, proven results, and partner relationships
- Multiplies reach and sales dramatically

**Ideal for**: Scaling after proven internal launches
[SOURCE: jeffwalker.com/jv-launch-vs-internal-launch-post/; productlaunchstrategy.org]

### Quick Launch

**When to use**: Existing product, need fast cash flow
**How it works**:
- Can be done in 4 days
- Takes only a couple of hours to set up
- Offer existing products to current email list

**Ideal for**: E-commerce, new product batches, quick revenue
[SOURCE: productlaunchstrategy.org/tag/the-quick-launch/]

### Evergreen Launch

**When to use**: Want to automate the launch process
**How it works**:
- Transforms a one-time launch into an automated ongoing process
- New subscribers enter the launch sequence automatically
- Replicates the live launch experience on autopilot

**Ideal for**: Passive income, scaling without live launches
[SOURCE: productlaunchstrategy.org/tag/evergreen-launch/]

### Live Launch

**When to use**: Want deeper engagement during prelaunch
**How it works**:
- Uses live broadcasts for prelaunch content
- Provides more value through real-time interaction
- Keeps audience engaged longer

**Ideal for**: Personality-driven brands, community building
[SOURCE: productlaunchclass.com PLF review]

## 3. The 9 Mental Triggers

Jeff Walker identifies 9 psychological triggers that influence decision-making. These are woven throughout every phase of the launch sequence.

### 1. Authority
- People gravitate towards and respect those who exude expertise
- Established through prelaunch content, teaching, credentials, results
- **How activated in PLF**: PLC1 positions you as the expert; your content demonstrates authority

### 2. Reciprocity
- "There's an innate human tendency to return a favor"
- When you give something of value, people feel compelled to give back
- **How activated in PLF**: Free valuable prelaunch content creates obligation

### 3. Trust
- Foundational to everything -- "building trust quickly is a game-changer"
- Trust is built through consistency, transparency, and delivering on promises
- **How activated in PLF**: Multiple touchpoints over days build familiarity and trust

### 4. Anticipation
- "Creating a buzz, like the excitement preceding a major product launch"
- Drives engagement and enthusiasm before the offer is even made
- **How activated in PLF**: Pre-prelaunch through PLC sequence builds anticipation over 7-10+ days

### 5. Likability
- "We lean towards doing business with individuals we find approachable and relatable"
- Being genuine and personable increases influence
- **How activated in PLF**: Personal stories, vulnerability, conversational tone throughout

### 6. Events & Rituals
- "Collective experiences leave lasting impressions"
- Making the launch feel like an event creates emotional investment
- **How activated in PLF**: The launch IS the event; cart open = premiere; cart close = deadline

### 7. Community
- "People act in ways they think their community expects them to act"
- Creating a sense of belonging influences behavior
- **How activated in PLF**: Comments, engagement, shared experience during prelaunch; Facebook groups; live Q&A

### 8. Scarcity
- "The allure of the limited is undeniable"
- Highlighting scarcity increases perceived value and urgency
- **How activated in PLF**: Limited cart window, bonuses that expire, price increases

### 9. Social Proof
- "People are more likely to follow the actions of others, especially in uncertain situations"
- Testimonials and reviews build credibility
- **How activated in PLF**: Case studies in PLC2, testimonials during cart open, "X people already enrolled" updates

[SOURCE: medium.com/@aleshadrew; sethdailey.com/post/nine-mental-triggers; "Launch" book; deliberatedirections.com/mental-triggers/]

## 4. The Sideways Sales Letter (Deep Dive)

### What It Is
A series of 3 pieces of prelaunch content (videos, emails, blog posts, livestreams) that replace the traditional long-form sales letter. Instead of one massive sales page delivered vertically, the content is delivered horizontally over 6-10 days.

### Why It Works (vs. Traditional Sales Letters)
- Traditional long-form sales letters have declining conversion rates
- People don't read 20-page sales letters anymore
- The old approach of splitting a sales letter into pieces still doesn't create value
- The Sideways Sales Letter actually TEACHES, INSPIRES, and SELLS over time
- Each piece is valuable on its own -- whether or not they buy

### The PSP Path (Problem-Solution-Problem)
- Differs from traditional PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
- Based on the insight that "every solution holds the seeds to the next problem"
- Each piece of content solves one problem and introduces the next
- This keeps prospects engaged through the entire sequence

### Key Principle
"The job of this Prelaunch Content is NOT to immediately get them to buy your product, but rather to take your prospect on a journey where you'll reveal the opportunity, transformation, and experience they'll get."

[SOURCE: productlaunchclass.com/sideways-sales-letter/; breakthroughmarketingsecrets.com; hubdip.com/blog/sideways-sales-letter/; "Launch" book]

## 6. Core Principles & Beliefs

### Foundational Beliefs

1. **Human nature doesn't change**: "Technology may change, but human nature doesn't... and hasn't... for thousands of years." The principles are psychological, not technological.

2. **Value before asking**: Give immense value before ever making an offer. This creates reciprocity and trust.

3. **Marketing should be a conversation, not a monologue**: "I didn't know how to sell so I romanced people... And it turned out that philosophy was really completely in alignment with where the world was going with the internet."

4. **Your list is your business**: The email list is the most important asset any online business owns. It provides control and independence.

5. **Launches are events**: "Turn your on-sale date into an EVENT that people look forward to." Making marketing an event creates buzz and excitement.

6. **The anti-selling approach**: "The most important factor in turning prospects into customers does not rely on selling at all. Instead, you want to ease into the sale by offering value, building the relationship, and making your prospect feel part of the creation process."

7. **Empathy is the ultimate superpower**: "The ability to shift perspective and understand the viewpoint of another person... is the ultimate entrepreneurial superpower."

8. **Focus is the scarcest resource**: Saying "no" to almost everything is the key to exceptional success.

9. **Principles over tactics**: The core strategies haven't changed since 2005. What works is timeless psychology, not trendy tactics.

10. **Co-creation with your market**: Don't build in isolation. Survey, ask, listen, and let your market shape the product.

## 7. Decision Heuristics -- When to Use Which Launch Type

### Launch Type Selection Matrix

| Situation | Recommended Launch | Why |
|-----------|-------------------|-----|
| No product, no list | **Seed Launch** | Co-create product, build first list, get paid to create |
| New product idea, existing business | **Seed Launch** | Validate before investing in full production |
| Have a list + proven product | **Internal Launch** | Fastest path to 6-7 figure months |
| Proven offer + want scale | **JV Launch** | Partners multiply reach exponentially |
| Need quick cash flow | **Quick Launch** | 4 days, minimal setup, existing products |
| Want passive/automated sales | **Evergreen Launch** | Automate the launch sequence |
| Want deeper engagement | **Live Launch** | Real-time interaction builds stronger bonds |

### The Progression Path (Circle of Awesome)

```
[1] Seed Launch
    -> Validates idea, creates product, builds first list
    -> Generates first testimonials and case studies

[2] Internal Launch
    -> Launches proven product to own list
    -> Polished sideways sales letter
    -> First layer of word-of-mouth buzz

[3] JV Launch
    -> Leverages proven funnel and results
    -> Partners promote to their lists
    -> Massive list growth and sales multiplication

REPEAT -> Each cycle compounds (new buyers, testimonials, partnerships)
```

### Launch Stacking (Advanced)

- Run multiple launches in sequence throughout the year
- Each launch compounds on the last
- Reported 134% additional revenue from stacking 3 smaller launches around 1 main annual launch
- Do 2-3 launches per year minimum
- Different products can be launched to the same list

## 8. Anti-Patterns -- What Jeff Walker Warns Against

### 1. Hope Marketing
"Just having a great product isn't enough. Hope Marketing is putting all the work in and creating something amazing, and then just hoping people will somehow magically find it and give you money."
**Instead**: Build a deliberate launch sequence with anticipation and urgency.

### 2. Creating Products Nobody Wants
The biggest reason for epic fails. People build products based on their own assumptions instead of asking their market.
**Instead**: Use Seed Launches. Survey your audience. "Enter the conversation already happening in their minds."

### 3. The Monologue Approach
Blasting your offer without context -- "Hey here I am now BUY!!"
**Instead**: Build value through a conversational prelaunch sequence.

### 4. Not Building a List First
Launching without an email list, or launching to a cold list.
**Instead**: Build and nurture your list before launching. "Your list is your most important asset."

### 5. Underestimating Audience Size Needed
"People don't realize just how many people they need to be in front of." You need volume AND trust to convert.
**Instead**: Grow your list continuously. Use JV partnerships for reach.

### 6. Assuming Your Audience's Knowledge Level
Presupposing prospects know things they don't, or speaking above their level.
**Instead**: "People want you to talk about them, not your product or business." Show you understand their world.

### 7. Skipping the Prelaunch
Going straight to selling without building anticipation, trust, or value.
**Instead**: The prelaunch IS the launch. The PLC sequence does 80% of the selling.

### 8. Not Debriefing After Launch
Failing to analyze what worked and what didn't.
**Instead**: Walker has a structured debrief process after every single launch.

### 9. Delayed Team Building
Walker's "single biggest mistake" -- not hiring or building a team soon enough, costing years of growth.
**Instead**: Invest in people earlier than feels comfortable.

### 10. Confusing Scarcity with Dishonesty
Fear of using scarcity/urgency because it feels "sleazy."
Walker's position: "It's not sleazy or slimy to remind them this offer is only open for 5 days -- you're being honest."
**Instead**: Use REAL scarcity. Real deadlines. Real limited bonuses. Honesty + urgency = ethical selling.

## SUMMARY: Agent Construction Quick Reference

### Voice Profile
- **Tone**: Warm, humble, conversational, teacher-first
- **Energy**: Calm, measured, coffee-with-a-friend pace
- **Style**: Story-driven, analogy-rich, no hyperbole
- **Structure**: Problem -> Story -> Concept -> Why -> How -> Proof

### Key Behavioral Rules for an AI Agent
1. Always lead with value before mentioning any offer
2. Use "conversation" framing, never "pitch" framing
3. Tell relatable stories before teaching principles
4. Reference the 9 mental triggers naturally (don't name them explicitly to prospects)
5. Use the vocabulary list above -- match his terminology exactly
6. Frame scarcity as honest, not manipulative
7. Always connect tactics back to timeless psychological principles
8. Be self-deprecating about early failures; genuine about success
9. Focus on the prospect's world, not the product's features
10. End teachings with actionable next steps, not hype
