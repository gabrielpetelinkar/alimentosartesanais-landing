## Action Items

### Phase 1: 3-Act Video Structure

**For Short-Form (15-60 seconds):**
```
ACT 1: THE HOOK (0-3 seconds)
├── Visual: [Specific first frame — what the viewer SEES]
├── Audio: [Opening sound/words — what they HEAR]
├── Text: [On-screen text overlay if applicable]
├── Goal: STOP the scroll. Create curiosity or tension.
└── Rule: If Act 1 doesn't work, nothing else matters.

ACT 2: THE JOURNEY (3-45 seconds)
├── The story unfolds: [Key beats of the narrative]
├── Visual variety: [Alternate between A-roll and B-roll]
├── Pacing: [Fast cuts for energy OR slow holds for emotion]
├── Tension: [Build toward the payoff]
└── Rule: Every shot must earn its place. Cut anything that doesn't serve the story.

ACT 3: THE PAYOFF (last 5-15 seconds)
├── Resolution: [The lesson, the result, the punchline]
├── Emotion: [What should the viewer FEEL?]
├── CTA: [What should they DO? (follow, comment, save)]
├── Loop potential: [Can the ending connect back to the beginning?]
└── Rule: End with EMOTION, not information.
```

**For Long-Form (3-20 minutes):**
```
ACT 1: THE HOOK + SETUP (0-2 minutes)
├── Cold open: Jump straight into the most compelling moment
├── Title/intro: Brief, branded (5-10 seconds max)
├── Context: Set up the story — who, what, why
├── Promise: What will the viewer learn/experience?
└── Rule: Front-load the best stuff. Don't save it for later.

ACT 2: THE JOURNEY (bulk of video)
├── Rising action: Build the story through scenes
├── Conflict/challenges: Show the struggle
├── Progress: Show the work, the process
├── B-roll: Rich supplementary footage
├── Talking head: Reflection, explanation, commentary
└── Rule: Alternate between action and reflection. Never stay in one mode too long.

ACT 3: THE PAYOFF + CLOSE (last 1-3 minutes)
├── Climax: The moment everything comes together
├── Lesson: What did we learn?
├── Emotion: End on a feeling, not a fact
├── CTA: Subscribe, watch next video, etc.
└── Rule: The ending determines how people REMEMBER your video.
```

```
#3: [Shot description] | Type: [...] | Duration: [X sec]
...

#1: [Location/scene setting] | Wide/drone/time-lapse
#2: [Context shot] | Medium shot of environment
...

TRANSITION SHOTS
#1: [Time-lapse / walking shot / creative transition]
#2: [Drone pull-out / door opening / screen wipe]
...
```

**B-Roll Strategy:**
- Rule: Film 3x more B-roll than you think you need
- Always capture: Hands working, details, environment, reactions, process
- "B-roll banks": Generic shots that can be reused across videos
  - Walking shots, typing, coffee making, workspace, tools of the trade
  - Outdoor establishing shots (sunrise, street, nature)
  - Screen recordings of relevant processes

### Phase 3: Editing Rhythm Map

```
EDITING RHYTHM: [Video Title]

SECTION 1 (0:00 - 0:XX): FAST
├── Cut pace: 2-3 seconds per shot
├── Purpose: Energy, attention, excitement
├── Music: Upbeat, driving
├── Use for: Hooks, montages, action sequences

SECTION 2 (0:XX - 0:XX): MEDIUM
├── Cut pace: 5-8 seconds per shot
├── Purpose: Storytelling, explanation
├── Music: Moderate, supportive
├── Use for: Main narrative, talking head with B-roll cutaways

SECTION 3 (0:XX - 0:XX): SLOW
├── Cut pace: 10+ seconds per shot
├── Purpose: Emotion, impact, reflection
├── Music: Atmospheric or silent
├── Use for: Key moments, revelations, emotional beats

SECTION 4 (0:XX - end): VARIABLE
├── Cut pace: Mix of fast and slow
├── Purpose: Build to climax
├── Music: Building intensity → resolution
├── Use for: Payoff, lesson, closing
```

**Music Direction:**

### Phase 4: Production Checklist

```
PRE-PRODUCTION
□ Story structure finalized (3 acts)
□ Shot list complete
□ Location(s) confirmed
□ Equipment charged and tested
□ B-roll list prepared

PRODUCTION
□ A-roll filmed (all talking head / main footage)
□ B-roll filmed (all supplementary shots)
□ Establishing shots captured
□ Transition shots captured
□ Audio check (clean, no background noise)
□ Backup footage verified

POST-PRODUCTION
□ Import and organize footage
□ Rough cut (assemble story in order)
□ Fine cut (tighten pacing, remove dead space)
□ Add B-roll (cover jump cuts, add visual interest)
□ Add music (sync to edit rhythm)
□ Add text/graphics (captions, titles, lower thirds)
□ Color correction (basic: exposure, white balance)
□ Audio mix (voice clear, music supportive, levels balanced)
□ Export at platform-native specs
□ Create thumbnail (if YouTube)
```

## Quality Gate

- [ ] 3-act structure clearly defined
- [ ] First 3 seconds designed as scroll-stopper
- [ ] Shot list has adequate A-roll and B-roll
- [ ] Editing rhythm mapped (not just "edit it together")
- [ ] Music/audio direction provided
- [ ] Production checklist adapted to creator's equipment level
- [ ] Video structure serves the story (not the other way around)

## Veto Conditions
- No clear story/narrative → STOP, structure the story first (go back to Phase 1)
- Hook doesn't stop the scroll → STOP, reengineer the first 3 seconds
- Shot list is just "film myself talking" → STOP, add visual variety
- No B-roll planned → WARN, B-roll makes the difference between amateur and professional
- Video is information-only (no emotion) → STOP, add emotional beats
