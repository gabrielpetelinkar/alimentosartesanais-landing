## Elicitation (Required)

### Step 1: Transformation Definition
1. "What is the specific transformation your student will achieve?"
2. "Where is your student right now (Point A)? Describe in detail."
3. "Where will they be after completing your course (Point B)? Be specific."
4. "How long should the transformation take? (4 weeks? 8 weeks? 12 weeks?)"

### Step 2: Milestone Mapping
1. "What are the major milestones between Point A and Point B?"
2. "What does the student need to learn or do FIRST before anything else?"
3. "What's the #1 thing that makes your approach different?"
4. "Where do most people get stuck? What's the hardest part?"

### Step 3: Format Decision

2. "Will this be self-paced or cohort-based?"
3. "What's your ideal course price point?"
4. "What bonuses could you include that solve adjacent problems?"

## Action Items

### Phase 1: Define the Transformation
Write one sentence:
"Students will go from [Point A — specific painful situation] to [Point B — specific desirable outcome] in [timeframe]."

This sentence is the North Star for every decision that follows.

### Phase 2: Map the Milestones
Identify 4-8 major milestones between Point A and Point B.
Each milestone represents a MODULE in your course.

Rules:
- Milestones should be sequential — each builds on the previous
- Each milestone is a measurable achievement the student can celebrate
- The first milestone should deliver a quick win (builds momentum)
- The last milestone should be the full transformation (Point B)

### Phase 3: Break Milestones into Lessons
For each module/milestone, create 3-5 lessons:

```
MODULE [X]: [Milestone Name]
Milestone: [What the student achieves by end of this module]

  Lesson X.1: [Concept or Skill]
    └── Action: [What student does after this lesson]
  Lesson X.2: [Concept or Skill]
    └── Action: [What student does after this lesson]
  Lesson X.3: [Concept or Skill]
    └── Action: [What student does after this lesson]
```

Rules for lessons:
- Each lesson teaches ONE concept or skill
- Each lesson ends with a specific action item (the DO)
- Hook → Teach → Show → Do → Recap format per lesson
- If a lesson doesn't move the student toward the transformation, cut it

### Phase 4: Design Quick Wins
Quick wins go in Modules 1-2. They give students early momentum.

A quick win should be:
- Achievable within 1-2 hours of completing the module
- Tangible — the student can SEE or FEEL the result
- Shareable — the student wants to tell someone about it
- Connected to the larger transformation

### Phase 5: Select Course Format

| Format | Duration | Modules | Price Range | Best For |
|--------|----------|---------|-------------|----------|
| Workshop | 1-3 hours | 2-4 | $97-$297 | Quick wins, first-time creators |
| Spotlight | 4-6 weeks | 4-6 | $297-$997 | Deeper dives, standalone products |
| Signature | 8-12 weeks | 6-12 | $997-$2,997+ | Complete systems, premium positioning |

### Phase 6: Remove the Fluff
Review the entire outline and ask for each element:
- Does this serve the transformation? (If no → cut it)
- Is this need-to-know or nice-to-know? (If nice-to-know → cut it or make it a bonus)
- Will this overwhelm the student? (If yes → simplify)
- Could this be a bonus instead of a core module? (If yes → move it)

## Veto Conditions
- More than 10 modules → STOP, simplify (students won't complete it)
- No action items per lesson → STOP, add practical exercises
- Course is an information dump (no transformation) → STOP, restructure around outcome
- No quick wins in Modules 1-2 → STOP, design early momentum builders
- Modules don't build sequentially → STOP, reorder for logical progression
