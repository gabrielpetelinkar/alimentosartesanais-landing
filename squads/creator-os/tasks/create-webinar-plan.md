## Elicitation (Required)

### Step 1: Webinar Foundation
1. "What course or product will you sell at the end of this webinar?"
2. "What is the transformation your course delivers?"
3. "Who is your ideal attendee? What are they struggling with right now?"
4. "What's the one thing you want attendees to believe by the end of the webinar?"

### Step 2: Teaching Content
1. "What are 3-5 key strategies, steps, or secrets you can teach during the webinar?"
2. "What stories or examples can you share for each teaching point?"
3. "What quick wins can attendees get DURING the webinar?"
4. "What common myths or mistakes can you debunk?"

### Step 3: The Offer
1. "What is included in your course? (Modules, bonuses, community, etc.)"
2. "What is the price? Do you have a special webinar-only offer?"
3. "What testimonials or social proof do you have?"
4. "What's the urgency element? (Deadline, limited spots, expiring bonuses)"

## Action Items

### Phase 1: Webinar Title and Registration Page

**Title Formula:** "How to [Desirable Outcome] Without [Pain Point]"

Examples:
- "How to Create Your First Digital Course Without Being a World-Class Expert"
- "How to Meal Prep 5 Family Dinners in 2 Hours Without Spending a Fortune"
- "How to Build a Profitable Online Business Without Quitting Your Day Job"

**Registration Page Elements:**
- Compelling title (the hook)
- Date and time
- 3-4 bullet points of what they'll learn
- Brief bio of the presenter
- Clear CTA: "Save My Spot" or "Register Now"

### Phase 2: Webinar Script Outline

**SECTION 1: POSSIBILITY (First 10-15 minutes)**
Purpose: Show viewers what is possible for THEM

1. Open with a bold promise or surprising statistic
2. Share YOUR story briefly — establish credibility and relatability
   - "Are you like me?" (relatability)
   - "Can you lead me?" (credibility)
   - "Is there a path I can follow?" (method)
3. Paint a picture of life AFTER the transformation
4. Set expectations for the webinar: "By the end, you'll know exactly how to..."

**SECTION 2: PATH (30-40 minutes — the bulk)**
Purpose: Deliver massive value through teaching pillars

Choose a pillar structure:
- "3 Steps to [result]"
- "5 Mistakes that keep you from [result]"
- "The [number] Secrets of [result]"
- "The [number] Myths about [topic]"

For each pillar:
- Teach actionable content — give real wins
- Use stories and examples to illustrate
- Plant seeds: "In my course, we go deeper on this..."
- Keep each pillar to 8-12 minutes

**SECTION 3: PROMOTION (15-20 minutes — start by minute 45-50)**
Purpose: Convert viewers into customers

1. Transition naturally: "Now if you want to go deeper..."
2. Present your course as the vehicle for the full transformation
3. Walk through what's included (modules, bonuses, support)
4. Show social proof (testimonials, student results)
5. Present pricing with value anchoring
6. Add urgency (deadline, limited bonuses, enrollment closing)
7. Clear CTA: "Click the link below to enroll now"

**TIMING RULE:** Most attendees leave after 60 minutes. Your promotion MUST start before minute 50.

### Phase 3: Pre-Webinar Email Sequence

| Email | Timing | Subject Line Approach |
|-------|--------|----------------------|
| Email 1 | At registration | "You're in! Here's what to expect..." |
| Email 2 | 3 days before | "Quick question before [webinar name]..." |
| Email 3 | 1 day before | "Tomorrow is the day — don't miss this" |
| Email 4 | 1 hour before | "We're going live in 60 minutes!" |
| Email 5 | At start | "We're LIVE — join now!" |

### Phase 4: Post-Webinar Email Sequence

| Email | Timing | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Email 1 | Within 1 hour | Replay link + recap of key insights |
| Email 2 | Day 2 | Testimonial/case study + link to offer |
| Email 3 | Day 3 | FAQ + objection handling |
| Email 4 | Day 4 | "Last chance" urgency + bonus reminder |
| Email 5 | Closing day | Cart close / final deadline email |

**Key insight:** As many sales come from post-webinar emails as from the live webinar itself.

## Veto Conditions
- No product or course to sell → STOP, create the product first
- Promotion starts after minute 55 → STOP, restructure to start by minute 45-50
- Teaching pillars are too superficial (no real value) → STOP, add actionable content
- No post-webinar email sequence → STOP, these emails generate half the sales
- Webinar is all teaching, no promotion → WARN, you must sell — this is a sales tool
- Webinar is all pitch, no teaching → STOP, trust comes from teaching real content
