## Action Items

### Phase 1: Product Conception for Perpetual

Validate that your product fits the perpetual model:

**Perpetual Product Checklist:**
- [ ] Solves an ONGOING problem (not seasonal or one-time)
- [ ] Target audience searches for this solution daily
- [ ] Price between R$197-R$997 for main product (ideal perpetual range)
- [ ] Clear transformation that can be communicated in one sentence
- [ ] Competition exists (competition = demand)
- [ ] Product can be delivered digitally at scale

**If product is launch-only today:**
- Adapt the offer for perpetual (remove launch-specific bonuses, add evergreen urgency)
- Create a perpetual version of the sales page (no countdown timers tied to specific dates)
- Build email sequences that work year-round

### Phase 2: Build the Perpetual Funnel

Follow the complete funnel architecture from build-perpetual-funnel.md:

1. **Lead Magnet** → Free value that showcases your method
2. **Landing Page** → Single-objective lead capture
3. **Welcome Email Sequence** → 5 emails over 5 days
4. **Sales Page** → 12-step architecture
5. **Checkout** → Optimized with order bump, PIX, boleto
6. **Cart Recovery** → 3 emails (1h, 24h, 48h)
7. **Upsell/Downsell** → Post-purchase value maximization
8. **Perpetual Nurture** → Ongoing emails (2-3/week)

### Phase 3: Layer Launch on Top of Perpetual

**The Complete Machine (Maquina Completa):**

```
PERPETUAL (365 days/year) — Your foundation
  └── Daily traffic → leads → sales
  └── Consistent revenue (previsibilidade)
  └── Data collection for optimization

LAUNCH (2-4x per year) — Your amplifiers
  └── Cash injection (picos de faturamento)
  └── Audience re-engagement
  └── Social proof generation at scale
  └── Non-buyers from launch → perpetual nurture
```

**How they feed each other:**
- Perpetual warms leads who convert more easily during launches
- Launch energy creates urgency that feeds back into perpetual
- Launch non-buyers enter the perpetual nurture sequence
- Launch bonuses can become perpetual upsells after the launch

### Phase 4: Traffic Strategy

**Month 1-2: Foundation**
- Start with R$30-50/day on Facebook/Instagram Ads
- Optimize for lead capture (not direct sales)
- Test 3 ad creatives, 2-3 audiences
- Target: positive ROAS by end of Month 2

**Month 3-4: Optimization**
- Scale winning ads to R$100-200/day
- Add retargeting campaigns
- Optimize sales page based on data
- Add organic layer (Stories 10x)

**Month 5-6: Scale**
- Scale to R$300-500/day (if ROAS > 2x)
- Add Google Ads and YouTube Ads
- Diversify traffic sources
- First launch overlay on top of perpetual

### Phase 5: Daily Execution Routine

**Morning (30 min):**
- Check yesterday's metrics (CPL, ROAS, sales, revenue)
- Review email open/click rates
- Diagnose any underperforming stage

**Midday (30 min):**
- Post Stories (if using organic strategy)
- Respond to DMs and comments
- Monitor ad performance

**Evening (30 min):**
- Adjust ad budgets based on day's performance
- Send scheduled emails or content
- Log daily numbers in tracking spreadsheet

**Weekly Review (1 hour):**
- Aggregate weekly metrics
- Identify optimization opportunities
- Plan next week's content and ads
- A/B test new elements (subject lines, ad copy, landing page)

### Phase 6: Scaling Roadmap

| Timeline | Revenue Target | Key Actions |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Month 1 | Break even | Launch funnel, start traffic, collect data |
| Month 2 | 1.5x ROAS | Optimize conversion points, fix leaks |
| Month 3 | 2x ROAS | Scale traffic, add organic layer |
| Month 4 | 2.5x ROAS | Add upsells, improve ticket medio |
| Month 5 | 3x ROAS | First launch overlay, diversify traffic |
| Month 6 | 3-5x ROAS | Full machine running, predictable revenue |

## Veto Conditions
- Product solves a seasonal or one-time problem → WARN, perpetual works best with daily demand
- No budget for ANY traffic (paid or time for organic) → STOP, need at least one traffic source
- Creator expects results in Week 1 → STOP, set realistic expectations (Month 2-3 for positive ROAS)
- No willingness to check metrics daily → STOP, perpetual requires daily monitoring
- Product price below R$97 → WARN, margins may be too thin for paid perpetual
- Creator wants to skip the funnel and just run ads to sales page → STOP, funnel stages matter
