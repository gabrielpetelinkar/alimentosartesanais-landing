## Action Items

### Phase 1: Define Your Digital Writing Compass
Map your content into 4 types:

| Type | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Actionable** | Teach how to do something | "5 steps to get food safety certified" |
| **Analytical** | Break down why something works | "Why 80% of artisanal producers fail compliance" |
| **Aspirational** | Share vision and motivation | "My journey from home kitchen to certified producer" |
| **Anthropological** | Observe patterns and trends | "The artisanal food revolution: what's changing in 2026" |

### Phase 2: Write 30 Atomic Essays (Ship 30 for 30)
Rules:
- 250-300 words each
- One idea per essay
- Write daily for 30 days
- Publish immediately (Practice in Public)
- Don't edit for perfection — publish for feedback

**Atomic Essay Structure:**
1. **Hook** (first line): Pattern interrupt or bold claim
2. **Setup** (2-3 sentences): Context and why it matters
3. **Body** (main content): The insight, steps, or story
4. **Takeaway** (last line): Clear conclusion or CTA

### Phase 3: Apply Rate of Revelation
Keep readers engaged by revealing information at the right pace:
- Don't dump everything in the first paragraph
- Each sentence should make them want to read the next
- Use open loops (hint at what's coming)
- Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
- One-line paragraphs for emphasis

### Phase 4: Analyze and Double Down
After 30 essays:
1. Which topics got the most engagement?
2. Which headlines performed best?
3. What format resonated (lists, stories, lessons)?
4. Double down on winners, drop losers

### Phase 5: Content-to-Product Pipeline
Every piece of content should connect to your products:
- Add a CTA to your free lead magnet in every post
- Repurpose Atomic Essays into course module previews
- Turn popular posts into email sequences
- Use comments/DMs as market research
