## Elicitation (Required)

### Step 1: Value Ladder Design
1. "What's your main product? (the course from Phase 1)"
2. "What could you offer for FREE that gives a quick win?"
3. "What's a low-ticket entry point? ($7-$47)"
4. "What's your premium/high-ticket offering? ($997+)"
5. "What 1-on-1 or done-for-you service could you offer at the top?"

### Step 2: Epiphany Bridge
1. "What's YOUR origin story? How did you discover this expertise?"
2. "What was your 'aha moment' — the epiphany that changed everything?"
3. "What was life like BEFORE the epiphany? (the old way)"
4. "What was life like AFTER? (the new way)"
5. "What's the emotional core of this story?"

### Step 3: Unique Mechanism
1. "What makes YOUR approach different from everyone else?"
2. "Why do existing solutions fail? What's missing?"
3. "What's the name of your unique process/framework/method?"

## Action Items

### Phase 1: Build the Value Ladder
```
TIER 1: BAIT (Free)
  → Lead magnet: [checklist/guide/mini-course]
  → Goal: Get them into your world
  → Example: "5-Step Food Safety Checklist"

TIER 2: FRONTEND ($7-$47)
  → Low-ticket offer: [ebook/workshop/template]
  → Goal: Turn lead into buyer
  → Example: "The Complete Food Safety Compliance Guide — $27"

TIER 3: MIDDLE ($97-$497)
  → Core offer: [your main course]
  → Goal: Deliver the transformation
  → Example: "Artisanal Food Production Masterclass — $297"

TIER 4: BACKEND ($997-$2997)
  → Premium offer: [group coaching/program]
  → Goal: Deeper transformation + community
  → Example: "Producer Accelerator — 12-week group program — $1,497"

TIER 5: HIGH-TICKET ($5K+)
  → Done-with-you or done-for-you
  → Goal: Maximum results
  → Example: "1-on-1 Business Setup — $5,000"
```

### Phase 2: Craft the Epiphany Bridge Story
Follow this structure:
1. **The Backstory**: Where were you before? What was your life like?
2. **The Wall**: What problem/frustration did you hit?
3. **The Epiphany**: What was the moment everything changed?
4. **The Framework**: What system/method did you develop?
5. **The Achievement**: What results did you get?
6. **The Mission**: Why are you teaching this now?

### Phase 3: Stack the Offer
The offer stack should make the price feel like a steal:

| Component | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Main Course | $X |
| Bonus 1: [Template/Toolkit] | $Y |
| Bonus 2: [Community Access] | $Y |
| Bonus 3: [Live Q&A Sessions] | $Y |
| Bonus 4: [Quick-Start Guide] | $Y |
| **Total Value** | **$XXXXX** |
| **Your Price** | **$XXX** |

Rule: Total perceived value should be 10x+ the actual price.

### Phase 4: Hook, Story, Offer
For every piece of marketing:
1. **HOOK**: Grab attention (pattern interrupt, curiosity, bold claim)
2. **STORY**: Connect emotionally (Epiphany Bridge or student success)
3. **OFFER**: Present the irresistible deal (stack + urgency + guarantee)

## Veto Conditions
- No unique mechanism/differentiation → STOP, find what makes you different
- Offer value below 5x price → STOP, add more value or reduce price
- No Epiphany Bridge story → WARN, write it before launching
- Pricing without value ladder context → STOP, build the ladder first
