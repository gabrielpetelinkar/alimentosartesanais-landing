## Action Items

### Phase 1: Define the Big Idea (Grande Ideia)

The Big Idea is NOT a promise. It's the final conclusion — a strong reasoning behind your theme.

**Process:**
1. Identify the conventional wisdom in your niche (what everyone says)
2. Find where the conventional wisdom is WRONG or incomplete
3. Formulate your contrarian truth (the Big Idea)
4. Build your entire copy around proving this Big Idea

**Example:**
- Conventional wisdom: "You need to do big launches to make money online"
- Big Idea: "Launches are a bonus. Real money comes from selling every single day."
- All copy then proves WHY perpetual > launch-only.

### Phase 3: Write the 12-Step Sales Page

**Step 1: Promessa (Headline)**
- Strong, clear, believable
- Avoid exaggerations ("Ganhe R$10k por mes" is dead)
- Use premise-based headlines: state a truth, not a fantasy
- Example: "Aprenda a vender seu produto digital todos os dias, mesmo comecando do zero"

**Step 2: Para Quem E / Para Quem NAO E**
- Explicitly state who should buy and who should NOT
- Excluding people INCREASES trust and conversions

**Step 3: Prova Social**
- Video testimonials (most powerful)
- Screenshot of results
- Numbers (students, revenue, transformations)
- Mix diversity of results

**Step 4: Historia**
- Your origin story with scenic details
- Summary parts (overview) + scenic parts (specific moments)
- Make it relatable — include the struggle before the method

**Step 5: Conteudo (Differentials)**
- Show WHAT the product covers, not HOW (create curiosity)
- List modules with benefit-driven descriptions
- Highlight what makes this different

**Step 6: Gancho para Oferta**
- Natural transition: "Tudo isso que eu te mostrei esta disponivel em..."
- Bridge from content to commercial offer

**Step 7: Oferta (Value Stack)**
```
Main product ........................ R$[value]
Bonus 1: [Name] .................... R$[value]
Bonus 2: [Name] .................... R$[value]
Bonus 3: [Name] .................... R$[value]

TOTAL VALUE: R$[sum]
YOUR INVESTMENT: R$[actual price]
or [X]x R$[installment]
```

**Step 8: Objecao**
- Address each major objection directly
- Use the format: objection → reframe → proof

**Step 9: Mais Prova**
- Additional testimonials (shorter, different angles)
- Volume reinforces trust

**Step 10: CTA com Reforco de Beneficio**
- Button text states the BENEFIT: "Quero comecar a vender todo dia"
- NOT just "Comprar" or "Adquirir"

**Step 11: Garantia**
- Minimum 7 days (Brazilian law — CDC)
- Recommend 15-30 days for higher perceived safety
- Make it prominent — don't hide in the footer

**Step 12: Despedida**
- Final message with light urgency
- "O preco pode mudar a qualquer momento"
- Last CTA

### Phase 4: Light Copy Principles

Apply these rules throughout ALL copy:
- Be interesting FIRST, persuasive second
- Use humor — laugh at yourself
- Write like you talk
- Short paragraphs — one idea per paragraph
- Lead with premises, not promises
- Use specific numbers (R$170 milhoes, not "muito dinheiro")
- Tell stories with scenic details
- Each piece of copy should be standalone

### Phase 5: Supporting Copy

**VSL Script Outline (if applicable):**
- Hook (0-30 seconds): Bold statement or question
- Problem (30s-2min): Agitate the pain
- Story (2-5min): Your origin story
- Solution (5-8min): Your method explained
- Offer (8-12min): Value stack and pricing
- CTA (12-15min): Clear next step

**Key Email Copy:**
- Welcome email 1: Lead magnet delivery + intro
- Cart recovery email 1: "Esqueceu algo?" + FAQ
- Nurture email (story-based): Lesson through a personal story

## Veto Conditions
- Copy uses exaggerated promises ("Ganhe R$10k facil") → STOP, rewrite with premises
- No Big Idea — just listing features → STOP, define the contrarian truth first
- Missing more than 3 of the 12 steps → STOP, all steps matter for conversion
- No avatar analysis → STOP, you can't write copy without knowing who you're talking to
- Copy sounds like everyone else in the niche → STOP, find your unique voice and Big Idea
- Guarantee hidden or absent → STOP, make it prominent
- CTA buttons say "Comprar" instead of a benefit → STOP, rewrite with benefit-driven CTAs
