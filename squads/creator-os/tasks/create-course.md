## Elicitation (Required)

### Step 1: Topic Validation
1. "From your organized knowledge, which topic excites you most to teach?"
2. "Does your audience already ask about this? How do you know?"
3. "Who is your ideal student? Describe them in detail."
4. "What is their Point A (before your course) and Point B (after)?"

### Step 2: Transformation Design
1. "What specific result will students achieve?"
2. "How long should the transformation take? (4 weeks? 8 weeks? 12 weeks?)"
3. "What are the key milestones along the way?"
4. "What's the ONE thing that makes your approach different?"

### Step 3: Format Decision
1. "What format works best for your content? (video, text, audio, worksheets)"
2. "Will this be self-paced or cohort-based?"
3. "What's your launch timeline?"

## Action Items

### Phase 1: Validate Topic (The 10% Edge)
Remember: You only need to be 10% ahead of your students.

1. **Survey your audience** (if you have one):
   - "What's your #1 challenge with [domain]?"
   - "If I created a course on [topic], would you be interested?"
   - "What would make this course a must-buy for you?"

2. **Research demand**:
   - Search for similar courses (competition = demand)
   - Check forums/groups for questions about this topic
   - Look at Amazon book reviews for pain points

3. **Pre-sell test** (optional but recommended):
   - Create a simple sales page describing the transformation
   - Offer early-bird pricing
   - If people buy before it exists, the topic is validated

### Phase 2: Design Transformation Curriculum
Use the **Module-Lesson-Action** framework:

```
MODULE 1: [Foundation]
  ├── Lesson 1.1: [Concept]
  │   └── Action: [What student does after]
  ├── Lesson 1.2: [Concept]
  │   └── Action: [What student does after]
  └── Lesson 1.3: [Concept]
      └── Action: [What student does after]

MODULE 2: [Core Skill]
  ├── Lesson 2.1: ...
  ...
```

Rules for curriculum design:
- Each module = one milestone in the transformation
- Each lesson = one concept or skill
- Each lesson ends with an action item
- No more than 6-8 modules (less is more)
- Bonus content goes OUTSIDE the main curriculum

### Phase 3: Create Content
For each lesson:
1. **Hook**: Why does this matter? What problem does it solve?
2. **Teach**: The concept, framework, or skill
3. **Show**: Example or demonstration
4. **Do**: Action item for the student
5. **Recap**: Key takeaway

### Phase 4: Beta Launch
1. Offer the course to 5-10 beta students at 50% off
2. Deliver live (not pre-recorded) for the beta
3. Collect feedback after each module
4. Refine based on real student experience
5. THEN record the final version

## Veto Conditions
- No audience validation → WARN (can proceed with beta but higher risk)
- Course is information dump (no transformation) → STOP, restructure around outcome
- More than 10 modules → STOP, simplify (students won't complete it)
- No action items per lesson → STOP, add practical exercises
